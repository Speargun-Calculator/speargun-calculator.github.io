Speargun Setup Calculator

Knowing your speargun's power capability is important for ensuring it's up to the task. Whether you're plugging pellagics or snooping snapper, you want piece of mind your shaft will penetrate and hold, landing you that once-in-a-lifetime fish.

In recent years spearos have been adopting roller and, to lesser extent, invert roller setups to improve their speargun's power. The obvious benifit to more power is the ability to and decrease barrel length, improving tracking in the water.

Although the power advantage of the roller speargun over a traditional railgun is well known anecdotally, it's a little harder to find quantitative data. Maybe the best resource to date has been Chris Coatesman's "Unrolling the Roller" article which documents his favoured setups. When it comes to invert roller spearguns there is no information available to my knowledge.

Don't get me wrong, there are good tutorials on how to setup an invert roller, but how does it compare numerically to a traditional rollergun? That's why this calculator was made.

The speargun setup calculator finds the force a roller speargun or invert roller speargun transfers into the shaft. Maybe more importantly, the calculator also allows you to compare the roller to a traditional speargun setup. The default traditional setup is a twin 16mm band 1200mm speargun, as is the most common "one gun to rule them all" setup oft adopted here on the Sunshine Coast, Australia.

This calculator allows you to see if a roller head conversion is justifiable for your railgun. Or perhaps, why a double roller may be overkill for most use cases.

Please note that the calculator is theoretical, is not calibrated against empirical data and does not account for energy losses such as friction losses or inefficiencies in the roller head.
